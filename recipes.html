Fougasse aux olives et pancetta

A classic from the South of France, this fougasse is rich with olive oil, savory olives, and pancetta. Based on a recipe taught at École Lenôtre, this version combines traditional fermentation with aromatic Provençal flair.

Ingredients

  • 200g poolish
  • 75g Elrond starter
  • 260g water
  • 2g yeast
  • 340g bread flour
  • 60g rye flour
  • 2g salt
  • 25g olive oil
  • 50g chopped olives
  • 50g cooked, cooled pancetta (small cubes)

Steps & Schedule

Day 1 – Morning

  • Prepare poolish (equal parts flour and water, pinch of yeast). Leave at room temperature.
  • Feed Elrond. Leave at room temperature.

Day 1 – Evening

  • 18:45: Mix flour + 250g water. Autolyse for 30–45 min.
  • 19:30: Add poolish, Elrond, yeast, salt, and olive oil. Knead 8–10 min.
  • Add pancetta and olives. Perform 4 gentle stretch & folds (avoid damaging dough).
  • 20:00: Transfer to lightly oiled bowl. Cold ferment (bulk) for 12h in fridge, covered with plastic wrap.

Day 2 – Morning

  • 06:30: Divide into 4 × 260g pieces. Shape into balls.
  • 07:00: Rest 20 min covered (détente).
  • 07:20: Flatten slightly, shape into ovals. Make 5–7 cuts in each and stretch slightly open.
  • 07:30: Final proof 45 min under linen (apprêt).
  • 08:15: Bake at 475°F (use steam if possible) for 15 min.
  • Finish: Brush with garlic-infused olive oil immediately after baking.

Pain Rustique Classique au Levain

A traditional French country bread from the École Internationale de Boulangerie, this rustic pain au levain balances strength, structure, and simplicity. Designed to highlight levain fermentation with minimal ingredients and hands-on technique.

Ingredients

  • 400g flour
  • 200g poolish
  • 100g Elrond (levain)
  • 280g water
  • 10g salt

Steps & Schedule

Mixing & Pétrissage

  • Combine all ingredients and mix.
  • Autolyse for 10 min, covered.
  • On a floured table, knead with energy: stretch, fold, and smack the dough to incorporate oxygen. Rotate 90° between folds. Repeat in 3 sessions over 20 min total, until dough has good structure and elasticity.
  • Rest dough a few minutes, then form a large ball.

Fermentation (Pointage)

  • Rest 1.5h at ~24°C.
  • Perform a 4-way fold.
  • Rest 30 min.
  • Perform another 4-way fold.
  • Ferment another 3h.

Shaping

  • Simple fold, then shape gently into balls (do not degas).
  • Rest 10 min.
  • Envelope fold, rotate, shape into bâtards with gentle tension.
  • Flour and score with 2 classic grignes.

Baking

  • Preheat oven to 450F.
  • Bake for 35 min.
  • Lower temp slightly for the last few minutes if needed, or else the top will be black.

Old Fashioned Slab

A hearty, grain-rich artisan bread inspired by Le Cordon Bleu’s traditional formula. This dense and flavorful loaf is built with a mix of flours, toasted seeds, and a robust stiff levain named Moria. Perfect for breakfast or pairing with cheese and charcuterie.

Ingredients

  • Grain Mix:
  • 100g Sunflower seeds
  • 100g Brown flaxseeds
  • 100g Water
  • Dough:
  • 200g Bread flour
  • 100g Spelt flour
  • 100g Whole-wheat flour
  • 50g Rye flour
  • 50g All-purpose flour
  • 15g Salt
  • 3g Fresh yeast
  • 300g Grain mix (prepared earlier)
  • 380g Water (room temp)
  • 300g Stiff levain (Moria)
  • To finish: Oats (QS)

Steps & Schedule

Morning

  • Toast grains at 350°F (175°C) for 4–5 min in convection oven.
  • Immediately plunge toasted grains in water, stir, and refrigerate to cool.

Midday

  • Place water, stiff levain, flours, and yeast in mixer with hook.
  • Mix on speed 1. After 30 sec, add salt. Mix 2–3 min more on speed 1.
  • Switch to speed 3 for 5 min. Add grain mix, mix 1 min on speed 1, then 3 until distributed.
  • Shape dough into smooth ball, cover and proof at 30°C for 45–60 min.

Afternoon

  • Degas dough, divide into 3x 420g pieces. Pre-shape into balls. Rest 10 min.
  • Shape slightly like eggs, place seam-up in linen-lined baskets. Final proof 45–60 min at 30°C.

Baking

  • Preheat oven at max temperature.
  • Score.
  • Steam oven (half a cup on bottom).
  • Bake: 475F for 18 min.
  • Rotate, bake 5–6 min more.

Artisan Loaf

This artisan-style loaf was created by Germ, using a hybrid levain method and slow fermentation. The technique was refined at the Cordon Bleu Sourdough Masterclass, with emphasis on layered fermentation and rustic depth. A mix of poolish, rye-fed Elrond, and flaxseed soaker create a deeply flavorful and textured bread.

Ingredients

  • Poolish:
  • 100g water
  • 100g bread flour
  • Pinch of instant yeast
  • Rye Elrond:
  • 50g rye flour
  • 50g water
  • 50g Elrond
  • Grains Mix (optional):
  • 30g flaxseeds
  • 40g warm water
  • Bread Dough:
  • 250g bread flour
  • 50g rye flour
  • 200g water
  • 7g salt
  • Poolish (200g)
  • Rye Elrond (150g)
  • Soaked flaxseeds (with water)
  • Oat flakes as toping

Steps & Schedule

Day 1 - Morning

  • Prepare poolish: mix 100g water, 100g bread flour, pinch yeast. Leave at room temp.
  • Mix Elrond with rye flour and water for the rye levain. Leave at room temp.

Day 1 - Evening

  • 18h00: Mix flours and water for autolyse (30 min).
  • Prepare flaxseed soaker: mix seeds with 40g warm water, rest 30 min. Note that this is optional.
  • 18h30: Add salt, poolish, rye Elrond, soaked seeds to autolysed dough. Mix well.
  • 19h00–21h30: Bulk ferment for 2.5h with stretch & folds at 19h00, 19h30, and 20h00.
  • 21h30: Pre-shape into a loose ball, rest 15 min.
  • Wet the top and dip in oat flakes.
  • 21h45: Final shape and place into floured banneton, top on bottom. Cold proof overnight in fridge.

Day 2 - Morning

  • 08h30: Bake in steam oven (bread mode) at 475°F (245°C) for 35 min. Score just before bake.
  • Cool fully before slicing.

Elrond – The Airy Levain

Born from the ancient essence of fruit — grapes and apples — Elrond is a living Elven relic over 30 years old. Its parents, lovingly named Owen, began their fermentation journey at the Cordon Bleu in Ottawa, where generations of pastry students have fed, nurtured, and baked with Elrond. It is bright yellow, intensely sour (pH below 5.5), and wildly bubbly — as alive as the legends of the First Age.

Care & Feeding

  • Ratio: 1:1:1 (starter:flour:water) for daily feeding.
  • Flour: Primarily bread flour, but versatile with rye.
  • Water: Always use spring or filtered water — never tap water.
  • Frequency: Feed every 7 days if refrigerated, daily at room temp.
  • Storage: Loosely sealed in the fridge. Let breathe weekly.
  • Reactivation: Two daily feedings at room temp will reawaken its magic.

Rye Transformation

Elrond easily transforms into a powerful levain de seigle for rustic rye breads:

  • 100g Elrond
  • 500g rye flour
  • 400g spring water

Mix and let ferment 24–48 hours at room temperature. The result is a deep, earthy sourdough perfect for hearty, traditional loaves.

Notes

  • Elrond’s airy strength makes it ideal for baguettes, fougasse, pain de campagne, or soft levain loaves.
  • Its sharp tang and fruity aroma lend depth and character to even the simplest dough.
  • Best used with long bulk fermentations and gentle stretch and folds.

Moria – The Earthbound Levain

Forged in the depths of fermentation, Moria is a dark and grounded levain named after the legendary dwarven halls. Created five years ago at Le Cordon Bleu Ottawa from grapes, apples, and cranberries, it has since become the foundation of all earthy breads made by generations of pastry students. Its parent, Derrick, passed on a deep, fruity complexity that now defines this levain. Used for the iconic Old Fashioned Slab and featured in the final patisserie exam, Moria represents tradition, patience, and strength.

Care & Feeding

  • Flour: Bread flour only.
  • Water: Always use spring or filtered water.
  • Feeding schedule: Once every 1–2 weeks if refrigerated.
  • Storage: Keep loosely covered in the fridge. Allow to breathe after long storage.
  • Reactivation: Feed daily at room temperature for 2–3 days before use in baking.

Usage & Tradition

  • Moria is ideal for dense, rustic breads with earthy character and chewy crumb.
  • Signature use: Old Fashioned Slab and other earthy loaves in the final Cordon Bleu exam.
  • Pairs well with rye flour, toasted grains, or smoked ingredients like pancetta or olives.

Notes

  • While less bubbly than Elrond, Moria is powerful and forgiving.
  • Its mellow acidity and fruit-forward complexity deepen with time.
  • Perfect for recipes that call for patience and slow fermentation.
