Franks (Lower/Middle Rhine, Late Antiquity–Early Middle Ages)
Closest distances
Frank (6.57), +Nemetes (4.073), +Quadi (4.46), +Pannonian (4.89)
Language/Culture
West Germanic confederation (Salian & Ripuarian branches), strongly Roman-influenced by the 5th c.

Who and where

  • Origin along the lower and middle Rhine (modern Netherlands, Belgium, western Germany).
  • Foederati and foes of the Roman Empire; later founded the Merovingian kingdom in Gaul.

Population size

As a confederation in late antiquity likely several hundred thousand people spread across the Rhine frontier and northern Gaul.

Interactions

  • With Rome: raiding, service as federates, then rulers of northern Gaul.
  • With neighbors: Saxons, Alemanni, Thuringians, Burgundians; heavy admixture with Gallo‑Romans.

What happened

By the 6th–8th centuries the Franks dominated much of Gaul and western Germany, evolving into the Carolingian Empire.

Nemetes (Upper Rhine, Roman era)
Closest distances
Nemetes (8.127), +Frank (4.073), +Cherusci (4.582)
Identity
Small Rhine‑frontier tribe, culturally Celtic–Germanic; capital at Noviomagus of the Nemetes (near modern Speyer).

Who and where

  • Lived on the Upper Rhine opposite the Palatinate; incorporated into the Roman province of Germania Superior.
  • Known from Roman sources and inscriptions; allied to Rome at various times.

Population size

Likely a small civitas on the order of tens of thousands.

Interactions

  • Trade and military service under Rome; neighbors included Vangiones, Triboci, and later Alemanni and Franks.

What happened

Gradual Romanization; by late antiquity the Nemetes were absorbed into Alemannic and then Frankish populations along the Rhine.
